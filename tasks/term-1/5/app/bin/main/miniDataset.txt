A Star is Born is a masterpiece with electric chemistry between its leads. The raw, heartbreaking story captivated me completely.

The Gray Man delivers adequate action with impressive locations. However, its thin plot brings nothing new.

Jurassic World: Dominion is a convoluted, disappointing mess. It loses the original's magic completely.

Everything Everywhere is a creative blast mixing martial arts and family drama. It's profoundly moving and hilarious.

Dune: Part Two is epic filmmaking with staggering visuals and immersive sound. It's intellectually stimulating and immensely entertaining.

Morbius offers a confusing, dull experience with bad dialogue. The action is poorly choreographed.

Don't Look Up has a great premise and all-star cast. The heavy-handed satire repeats its point too much.

Black Widow is a standard Marvel origin with good action. It's entertaining but ultimately forgettable.

Spider-Man: No Way Home brings pure joy with brilliant multiverse execution. It balances fan service with emotional weight perfectly.

Red Notice is slick with decent chemistry between stars. It's entirely predictable yet enjoyable once.

Top Gun: Maverick masterfully blends nostalgia with fresh thrills. The breathtaking aerial sequences make it exhilarating cinema.

The Last Airbender is a terrible adaptation with wooden acting. It disrespects the source material entirely.

Cats is a bizarre failure with creepy CGI characters. The thin plot creates a confusing mess.

Parasite is a flawless genre-bending masterpiece with superb acting. It's clever, unsettling, and utterly captivating.

Puss in Boots: The Last Wish offers vibrant animation and mature themes. This hilarious adventure appeals to all ages.

John Wick: Chapter 4 delivers balletic, brutal action choreography. Each inventive set piece builds to an epic finale.

Doctor Strange has horror-tinged moments and visual flair. The rushed plot feels like a detour.

Paddington 2 overflows with charm, warmth, and genuine kindness. It's a whimsical, delightful film that restores faith.

Mad Max: Fury Road is an adrenaline rush of stunning visuals. The brilliantly choreographed chase is pure art.

The Batman provides moody atmosphere and excellent cinematography. However, it feels overly long.

The Rise of Skywalker is a frustrating, messy conclusion. Contrived plot points create disappointment.
